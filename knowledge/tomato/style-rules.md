# Tomato Style Rules

Purpose: guide drafting and review for Tomato-style commercial web fiction. These are editing principles, not rigid laws.

## Core Biases

- Start with pressure, desire, or danger instead of explanation.
- Make the protagonist's immediate problem visible within the opening scene.
- Let the protagonist act, choose, scheme, resist, or pay a cost in each chapter.
- Keep conflict concrete: a person, deadline, resource, secret, humiliation, threat, or opportunity.
- Deliver emotional payoff frequently. Payoff may be face-slapping, protection, promotion, romance progress, clue reveal, resource gain, or status reversal.
- End chapters with forward pull: a question, reveal, decision, arrival, threat, or unresolved confrontation.

## Use With Care

- Not every chapter needs a loud face-slap.
- Fast pacing does not mean skipping motivation.
- A familiar trope still needs a specific protagonist fantasy.
- A strong hook can be emotional, strategic, romantic, or mysterious; it does not have to be combat.
