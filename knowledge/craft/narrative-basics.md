# Narrative Basics

These notes are stable craft guidance for planning, drafting, and reviewing.

## Scene

A scene should usually contain:

- A character who wants something.
- Resistance or uncertainty.
- A change in information, status, emotion, or options.

## Character

Useful characters have:

- A current desire.
- A pressure point.
- A reason for their behavior.
- A relationship to the protagonist's fantasy or conflict.

## Long-Form Shape

Plan many chapters ahead, but commit canon one chapter at a time. A long novel needs repeated renewal:

- New enemies or obstacles.
- Higher stakes.
- Deeper relationship pressure.
- Reframed goals.
- Recovered or transformed foreshadowing.
