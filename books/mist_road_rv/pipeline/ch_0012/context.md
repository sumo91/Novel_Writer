## Craft Knowledge Cards

### Hard Rules

- craft_anti_infodump_opening: Open with a concrete scene pressure, then reveal only the rules the scene has made necessary.
  - Scope: craft
  - Severity: hard
  - Use when: A chapter opening, book opening, unit opening, or synopsis risks explaining setting before creating desire.
  - Check: Name the first visible action, conflict, or pressure before any world-rule explanation.
  - Check: List which setting, backstory, power-system, economy, or faction explanations can be delayed.
  - Check: Confirm each explanation is triggered by a scene event, object, consequence, or dialogue move.
  - Failure mode: Frontloaded worldbuilding
  - Failure mode: System manual before scene pressure
  - Failure mode: Backstory before reader curiosity
  - Failure mode: Clear but not desirable synopsis
- craft_golden_finger_engine: A golden finger is a story-serving advantage that rationalizes why the protagonist can grow, win, or access rare opportunities; it works best when it answers necessity and uniqueness, gives credible access to a hard arena, and creates a repeatable engine instead of pressing one button to win.
  - Scope: craft
  - Severity: hard
  - Use when: A story uses a system, cheat, inheritance, mentor, artifact, future knowledge, special tool, cognitive edge, special relationship, or other golden-finger mechanism to help the protagonist overcome pressure.
  - Check: Define the concrete困境 the protagonist could not reasonably face without this advantage.
  - Check: {'Answer necessity': 'who or what created this advantage, why it must exist, and what problem or agenda it serves beyond making the protagonist lucky.'}
  - Check: {'Answer uniqueness': 'why this protagonist receives or can use the advantage instead of any other person, and what that special fit does for the wider plot.'}
  - Check: State whether the mechanism gives a seat at the table, a growth path, a survival margin, information advantage, production capacity, or access to a special arena.
  - Check: If the mechanism is cognitive, specify whether it adds knowledge, deepens understanding, provides exclusive experience, improves talent, turns small actions into insight, or lets the protagonist safely use knowledge others fear.
  - Check: Keep cognitive superiority within the reader's grasp or build a clear logic chain; unreadable high cognition weakens identification and narrows the audience.
  - Check: Place the mechanism on a logic chain such as origin -> talent -> cognition -> ability -> result -> resource/status/influence -> new challenge, then decide which node the current arc is exploiting.
  - Check: Match the advantage to the genre stage; commercial, cultivation, horror, survival, and business stories need different forms of leverage.
  - Check: Keep the initial advantage strong enough to attract interest but limited enough that risk, planning, skill, teamwork, or tradeoffs still matter.
  - Check: Build upgrade paths, replacement stages, or new applications so the mechanism can keep pace with stronger future困境.
  - Check: Give the mechanism a suitable stage where its narrow function becomes valuable, rather than explaining functions in isolation.
  - Check: Reveal the mechanism through immediate scene pressure and visible use before full rules, menus, origin lore, or setting lectures.
  - Check: Avoid using the same function to solve consecutive困境 without adaptation, cost, cooldown, setup, or protagonist growth.
  - Failure mode: Advantage is so large that no credible困境 remains.
  - Failure mode: Mechanism exists only for装逼 and does not justify how the protagonist survives or grows.
  - Failure mode: No necessity or uniqueness; the advantage could be handed to anyone without changing the story.
  - Failure mode: Golden finger is mismatched with the story arena, such as combat leverage in a business story with no business effect.
  - Failure mode: Cognitive edge relies on concepts the reader cannot follow and therefore produces admiration without immersion.
  - Failure mode: Opening explains panels, rules, origin, or world lore before showing why the mechanism matters.
  - Failure mode: Same button solves every chapter, making protagonist judgment unnecessary.
  - Failure mode: Mechanism has no upgrade path, replacement plan, or escalating application after the first arc.
- craft_outline_minimum_map: Every outline layer should state its conflict, turning point, payoff, ending pull, and protagonist change in language the writer can follow.
  - Scope: outline
  - Severity: hard
  - Use when: A book, volume, arc, or unit outline risks becoming a list of facts instead of a map for writing.
  - Check: Name the layer's central conflict.
  - Check: Name the turning point or reversal.
  - Check: Name the payoff, climax, or stage end.
  - Check: Name the protagonist growth or pressure shift.
  - Check: Name how the next lower layer inherits the map.
  - Failure mode: Map without motion
  - Failure mode: Facts without arc
  - Failure mode: Character list instead of stage plan
- craft_protagonist_agency: Each chapter should force the protagonist to make a visible choice under pressure.
  - Scope: craft
  - Severity: hard
  - Use when: A chapter could become negotiation, exposition, or reaction without a decisive protagonist move.
  - Check: Name the protagonist's concrete choice.
  - Check: Name the pressure that makes the choice costly.
  - Check: Confirm the choice changes information, leverage, resources, or relationships.
  - Failure mode: Passive protagonist
  - Failure mode: Generic opposition
### Soft Heuristics

- craft_resource_pressure: Resource growth should create new costs, scarcity, exposure, or obligation.
  - Scope: craft
  - Severity: soft
  - Use when: The protagonist gains money, goods, power, allies, or information across a unit.
  - Check: Identify the cost or risk attached to each major gain.
  - Check: Check whether gains are balanced by spend, debt, danger, or harder opposition.
  - Failure mode: Resource inflation
  - Failure mode: Formulaic payoff
- craft_thread_economy: A unit should advance or pay off existing promises before opening too many new ones.
  - Scope: craft
  - Severity: soft
  - Use when: A unit opens new promises, mysteries, goods, factions, or negotiations.
  - Check: List which open threads this chapter advances.
  - Check: List which promises are newly opened.
  - Check: Confirm at least one prior thread is advanced, paid off, or explicitly deferred.
  - Failure mode: Thread sprawl
  - Failure mode: False cliffhanger

## Style Cards

- style_comic_timing: Comedy should land through timing, contrast, character logic, and consequence; it should not erase stakes or turn everyone into the same joke voice.
  - Use when: A scene uses humor, banter, absurd contrast, embarrassment, or light comedic relief.
  - Check: Put the joke after a clear setup, pressure, or expectation.
  - Check: Let the humor reveal character, status, or coping strategy.
  - Check: Return to the scene consequence after the laugh.
  - Failure mode: Random quips that pause the plot
  - Failure mode: Everyone speaking with the same joke rhythm
  - Failure mode: Humor undercutting an important emotional beat
- style_daily_uncanny_contrast: Make the uncanny stronger by placing it beside practical routine, money, food, family, work, paperwork, errands, or familiar rooms.
  - Use when: A scene should make ordinary life feel slightly unsafe, strange, haunted, secretive, or connected to hidden rules.
  - Check: Anchor the scene in one ordinary task or object before the abnormal pressure appears.
  - Check: Let the abnormal detail disturb a routine rather than floating as pure atmosphere.
  - Check: Preserve practical consequences: time, money, reputation, safety, family, or work.
  - Failure mode: Spooky atmosphere detached from daily life
  - Failure mode: Ordinary detail that never affects tension
  - Failure mode: Horror that erases practical character concerns
- style_dialogue_as_probe: Important dialogue should test information, shift leverage, expose a contradiction, or force the next choice.
  - Use when: Characters speak under suspicion, negotiation, threat, interrogation, or group pressure.
  - Check: Could this line be removed without changing information, leverage, or pressure?
  - Check: Does at least one speaker hide, test, reframe, or corner someone?
  - Check: Does the exchange reveal temperament through tactics instead of explanation?
  - Failure mode: Dialogue used only to explain setting
  - Failure mode: Characters politely taking turns stating facts
  - Failure mode: Suspicion scene with no tactical pressure
- style_face_slap_escalation: Face-slap payoff works best when pressure escalates in public, the opponent commits to a wrong judgment, and the protagonist's reversal is specific and earned.
  - Use when: A chapter uses humiliation, public challenge, underestimation, status reversal, or visible justice as the payoff engine.
  - Check: Establish who misjudges the protagonist and what they stake on that judgment.
  - Check: Escalate pressure before the reversal instead of resolving too early.
  - Check: {'Make the reversal concrete': 'proof, result, resource, witness, authority, or consequence.'}
  - Failure mode: Opponent is stupid before the protagonist acts
  - Failure mode: Payoff is only verbal dominance
  - Failure mode: Public pressure without visible consequence
- style_folk_reversal: The story should read like a clear, memorable incident: simple setup, concrete trouble, a small planted detail, and a final turn that reveals human nature or causal justice.
  - Use when: A scene or short arc uses a plainspoken anecdote, folk-ethics twist, coincidence, small secret, human-nature reveal, or compact reversal.
  - Check: State the central incident early and plainly.
  - Check: Plant one ordinary detail that later changes meaning.
  - Check: Make the ending turn reveal cause, character, debt, kindness, greed, irony, or consequence.
  - Check: Keep the lesson implicit; let the event carry the aftertaste.
  - Failure mode: Moral stated before the story earns it
  - Failure mode: Coincidence without planted detail
  - Failure mode: Too many named characters for a compact anecdote
  - Failure mode: Literary flourish that slows the incident
- style_grounded_trade: Use concrete goods, prices, stock limits, risk, and bargaining moves to carry tension.
  - Use when: Trading, negotiation, shopkeeping, resource exchange, or price pressure carries the scene.
  - Check: Name what is being traded or withheld.
  - Check: Show the cost, scarcity, exposure, or obligation attached to the gain.
  - Check: Let dialogue reveal leverage rather than explaining the leverage in narration.
  - Failure mode: Abstract negotiation
  - Failure mode: Empty business talk
  - Failure mode: Payoff without cost
- style_hook_with_consequence: A hook should point to a specific consequence, decision, reveal, danger, or obligation that the next scene must handle.
  - Use when: Checking chapter endings, unit endings, or scene breaks.
  - Check: Identify the concrete unresolved consequence at the end.
  - Check: Prefer action, rule loophole, identity shift, threat, or costly choice over vague foreboding.
  - Check: Ensure the hook connects to a next obligation rather than a decorative question.
  - Failure mode: Generic sense that things are not simple
  - Failure mode: Ending after payoff with no new pressure
  - Failure mode: Cliffhanger that does not change the next action
- style_layered_revelation: Reveal knowledge in layers: first name the anomaly, then give a usable partial explanation, then let later information change the meaning of what the reader already knows.
  - Use when: A chapter introduces mystery knowledge, hidden history, power rules, old documents, organizations, prophecy, or a clue that should matter again later.
  - Check: Does this chapter reveal only the layer needed for the current decision?
  - Check: Does the new answer create a sharper question or future obligation?
  - Check: Can an old clue, document, title, object, or phrase gain new meaning later?
  - Failure mode: Full encyclopedia explanation at first contact
  - Failure mode: Mystery name without later usable meaning
  - Failure mode: Reveal that closes curiosity without opening consequence
- style_lineage_memory: Lineage stories need memory with weight; today's choice should alter inheritance, reputation, debt, territory, marriage, teaching, or a future generation's options.
  - Use when: A story tracks family, clan, sect branches, inheritance, land, bloodline, genealogy, marriage ties, duty, rank, or multi-generation consequences.
  - Check: Name which family, branch, elder, junior, ally, or dependent is affected.
  - Check: Tie resources and status to durable obligations.
  - Check: Record consequences that should matter beyond the current chapter.
  - Failure mode: Clan names with no operational difference
  - Failure mode: Elders and juniors treated as interchangeable extras
  - Failure mode: Family benefit or harm forgotten after one scene
- style_mythic_consequence: Mythic texture should make choices feel larger than the moment, but every grand image must attach to a concrete rule, cost, wound, debt, or irreversible change.
  - Use when: A scene needs xianxia scale, fate pressure, ancient rules, cultivation danger, oath, sect, artifact, or cosmic consequence.
  - Check: Tie elevated imagery to a practical consequence.
  - Check: Use ritual, oath, rank, terrain, artifact, or taboo to carry scale.
  - Check: Keep protagonist agency visible inside the larger force.
  - Failure mode: Purple prose without consequence
  - Failure mode: Grand nouns replacing clear action
  - Failure mode: Fate pressure that makes characters passive
- style_official_power_pressure: Official power should create pressure through procedure, jurisdiction, paperwork, rank, discretion, and the gap between written rules and lived reality.
  - Use when: A scene involves a job title, badge, checkpoint, inspection, permit, office hierarchy, regulation, gray fee, public duty, or institutional loophole.
  - Check: Make the character's authority or limitation concrete.
  - Check: Show who benefits from the rule and who pays the hidden cost.
  - Check: Let the protagonist use, bend, expose, or reform a procedure with consequences.
  - Failure mode: Authority used as generic dominance
  - Failure mode: Rules named but not operationalized
  - Failure mode: Corruption exists only as mood, not mechanism
- style_plain_life_observation: Let ordinary life carry meaning through exact observation, useful detail, habit, craft, food, weather, and small human behavior; do not explain the feeling too loudly.
  - Use when: A scene needs plain literary life texture, local color, food, craft, old street memory, quiet character sketch, or restrained human warmth.
  - Check: Use one concrete local or sensory detail that changes how the person or place is understood.
  - Check: Show character through habit, trade, speech rhythm, or small repeated action.
  - Check: Let emotion remain slightly under-said; trust the detail to carry the aftertaste.
  - Check: Keep the sentence plain unless a richer phrase earns its place through precision.
  - Failure mode: Fake literary haze
  - Failure mode: Empty nostalgia
  - Failure mode: Local nouns piled up without observation
  - Failure mode: Directly explaining the feeling the detail should carry
- style_power_with_cost: Power should carry source, condition, limitation, cost, misuse risk, identity pressure, or long-term consequence; otherwise it becomes decoration or free convenience.
  - Use when: A character gains, uses, studies, trades, or explains special power, cultivation method, ritual, artifact, system reward, or supernatural rule.
  - Check: Name what enables the power and what limits it.
  - Check: Show one cost, danger, cooldown, material, taboo, debt, exposure, or corruption risk.
  - Check: Let power use change the next decision rather than ending pressure completely.
  - Failure mode: Free power without cost
  - Failure mode: Ability used as arbitrary solution
  - Failure mode: Risk mentioned once and forgotten
- style_question_chain: Keep reader pull active by turning each answer into a sharper next question.
  - Use when: A scene depends on mystery, unknown rules, hidden identity, or tactical uncertainty.
  - Check: Name the immediate question driving the scene.
  - Check: Ensure at least one answer creates a new risk, restriction, or contradiction.
  - Check: Avoid resolving a mystery into comfort without planting the next concrete problem.
  - Failure mode: One static mystery repeated without movement
  - Failure mode: Vague unease without a question
  - Failure mode: Answer that stops momentum
- style_risk_ledger_growth: Growth should read like a risk ledger; every gain needs a source, conversion path, exposure risk, and next-stage pressure.
  - Use when: A story uses cautious growth, hidden assets, two-world arbitrage, cultivation resources, low-profile advancement, or staged breakthroughs.
  - Check: Name the resource gained, spent, hidden, traded, or protected.
  - Check: Show why the protagonist avoids unnecessary exposure.
  - Check: Attach each advancement to a new risk, obligation, shortage, enemy attention, or logistics problem.
  - Failure mode: Caution becomes passive waiting
  - Failure mode: Resource gain has no later accounting
  - Failure mode: Breakthrough arrives without preparation, cost, or exposure risk
- style_rule_pressure: A rule should change what characters can safely do and should have a visible cost when misunderstood or broken.
  - Use when: A chapter uses explicit rules, contracts, game constraints, taboos, or supernatural operating limits.
  - Check: State the rule through pressure, demonstration, or consequence rather than detached explanation.
  - Check: Show who benefits from the rule and who is trapped by it.
  - Check: Make the rule create a decision, loophole, sacrifice, or countdown.
  - Failure mode: Rule as decorative lore
  - Failure mode: Rule explained before the scene creates need
  - Failure mode: No visible consequence for breaking or misunderstanding the rule
- style_scene_specificity: Anchor emotion and pressure in visible objects, gestures, spatial constraints, and immediate choices.
  - Use when: A chapter risks sounding abstract, summary-like, or over-explained.
  - Check: Replace generic mood labels with one concrete sensory or action detail.
  - Check: Make the current physical constraint visible.
  - Check: Show emotion through what a character notices, avoids, touches, buys, hides, or risks.
  - Failure mode: Empty atmosphere
  - Failure mode: Generic inner monologue
  - Failure mode: Summary instead of scene
- style_sword_action_pressure: Sword action should combine posture, distance, timing, body cost, and character attitude; the sharp line matters because the blade situation makes it dangerous.
  - Use when: A scene needs sword action, duel pressure, martial posture, sharp banter, body cost, technique reveal, or a compact combat reversal.
  - Check: Track distance, stance, weapon hand, wound, breath, or timing.
  - Check: Let technique reveal change the opponent's expectations.
  - Check: Keep banter tied to leverage, fear, contempt, or resolve.
  - Failure mode: Cool technique names without spatial consequence
  - Failure mode: Banter that pauses danger instead of sharpening it
  - Failure mode: Victory without bodily cost or tactical turn
- style_systemic_payoff: Systemic payoff works when a visible procedure, tool, team, rule, or institution improves and then changes what the story can do next.
  - Use when: A chapter payoff comes from institution building, technology, logistics, governance, training, infrastructure, or group coordination.
  - Check: Identify the system being improved, not just the hero's personal gain.
  - Check: Show the practical bottleneck before the upgrade lands.
  - Check: Let the upgrade create maintenance, governance, talent, enemy, or scaling pressure.
  - Failure mode: Technology or policy appears as exposition only
  - Failure mode: Group effort has no named roles or visible work
  - Failure mode: Upgrade solves everything without a new organizational cost
- style_voice_distinction: Each recurring character should sound like their role, pressure, knowledge, and temperament.
  - Use when: A scene has two or more recurring characters in dialogue.
  - Check: Remove speaker names and see whether the line still suggests who spoke.
  - Check: Give each major character a default pressure tactic.
  - Check: Avoid giving the protagonist generic dominance language unless the style bible permits it.
  - Failure mode: Interchangeable dialogue
  - Failure mode: Generic霸总 voice
  - Failure mode: Exposition disguised as dialogue
- style_warm_specificity: Warmth should come from specific care, small useful actions, shared routines, and remembered details rather than generic softness.
  - Use when: A scene should feel warm, lived-in, domestic, companionable, or emotionally restorative.
  - Check: Show one concrete act of care, repair, feeding, waiting, noticing, or making space.
  - Check: Anchor comfort in objects, habits, places, or repeated gestures.
  - Check: Let warmth coexist with a small pressure or cost so the scene still moves.
  - Failure mode: Empty cozy atmosphere
  - Failure mode: Characters being nice without choice or cost
  - Failure mode: Sentimental summary instead of lived detail
